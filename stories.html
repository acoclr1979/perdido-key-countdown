⭐ Legend
The Great Beach Ball Incident
Told by Christy & Adam  ·  ~2021

It started with a simple idea. Christy arrived at Perdido Key with what can only be described as a weather balloon — a six-foot inflatable beach ball, still in its shrink wrap, impossibly optimistic about the future.

Packing it had been its own odyssey. The box consumed half the car. Someone's knees were against it the entire drive down.

Then came the inflation. What followed was nearly an hour of rotating tag-team lung labor — adults taking shifts, faces cycling through colors not found in nature, at least one near-medical event involving dizziness and hyperventilation. A pump materialized at some point. It helped less than anyone hoped. The ball grew. Slowly. Stubbornly. Like it was doing us a favor.

But finally — finally — it was done. Six feet of pure vinyl ambition. The kind of beach presence that makes strangers lower their sunglasses.

We walked it to the water's edge. The whole family gathered. Someone said something that felt cinematic in the moment. We released it.

It was airborne in two seconds. Gone in four.

The Gulf wind took it east at what witnesses conservatively estimated as 100 miles per hour. We watched it clear the beach. Clear the dunes. Clear the tree line. It did not slow. It did not waver. It did not look back.

No one chased it. There was nothing to chase.

To this day its whereabouts are unknown. It may be in Georgia. It may be in the Atlantic. It may still be moving.

The ball was never recovered. The hyperventilation was.

Actual size. We are not exaggerating.

🌽 Tournament
Perdido Cornhole Championship
Champions: Greg & Jack Crane  ·  2026

What started as a carefully balanced nine-team tournament ended with an unforgettable family showdown. The field was seeded to even out skill levels, a play-in game set the final bracket spot, and eight teams moved into single elimination.

Play-In
Parker & Dane defeated Brady & Carey to earn the final quarterfinal spot.
Quarterfinals
Aidan O. & Cam defeated Aiden & Kasey
Grayson & Ella Crane defeated Parker & Dane
Greg & Jack defeated Rick & Jennifer J.K.
Bill & Amalia defeated JJ & Adam
Semifinals
Grayson & Ella Crane defeated Aidan O. & Cam
Greg & Jack defeated Bill & Amalia
Championship
Greg & Jack defeated Grayson & Ella Crane to capture the title.

The story everyone will remember: the championship turned into an unexpected Crane family showdown. On one side stood Greg & Jack Crane. On the other was Ella Crane, partnered with Grayson — widely regarded as the tournament favorite, a player who competes at an extremely high level. Most expected Grayson to finish the job. Instead, Greg & Jack played lights out, pulled off the biggest upset of the day, and walked away with the title.

Sometimes cornhole reminds us why we play the games. Seedings matter. Experience matters. But on any given afternoon, great teamwork, clutch shots, and a little momentum can rewrite the script.
